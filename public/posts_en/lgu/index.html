🐉🧱 CUHKSZ Survival Handbook

December 9, 2024

CUHKSZ Survival Handbook

(under construction)

  • CUHKSZ has a short history, and in my view it is far from an ideal school — especially because of weak registry administration, uneven teaching quality across professors, and some expensive but low-value institutional designs such as TUT.

  • Physical exams / fitness tests: the university often publishes precise time slots for each person, but in practice those schedules are not strictly enforced. For physical exams, simply bring the form and go early; avoid noon because the hospital staff take lunch breaks. For fitness tests, arriving outside the assigned time may still work if you report your original slot at the late check-in desk.

  • Strange teaching-building layout: from the third floor upward, the TB and TC buildings are effectively mirrored, so locations such as TB6 may actually be found inside the TC building. This seems to imitate the layout of the Shatin CUHK campus. Also, the first level visible from Lower Campus Avenue in TX is effectively the “second floor.”

  • Running-check app: 5% of every PE course grade comes from distance check-ins, each about two kilometers — slightly longer than the walk from Upper Campus to Lower Campus or from Shaw College to TX.Shaw College to TX. In practice, students can still complete the check-in partly by using the campus bus. Non-iOS phones are especially prone to app bugs: after switching away from the running app and using other location-tracking apps, the recorded path may drift or vibrate in place, and sometimes the bug is severe enough to complete much of the run without real movement.徘徊振动，有时甚至能直接靠此 bug 原地不动直接完成乐跑。

  • Weak school systems: sis is a rather crude and old-fashioned website. It is used for course registration, yet students often still fail to get courses because the university does not properly estimate demand, and some courses reserve additional seats for particular schools, making the whole system chaotic. In many cases, petition forms are still needed to enter a class. OJ, used by SDS for homework and exercises, also has many unresolved issues: it cannot fully prevent “coding to the visible answer,” often gives inadequate storage space and compute time, and supports very few packages beyond util.

  • Fruit: whole fruit is usually available at Tianmao in Upper Campus; Shaw College second floor and Happy Dining both sell self-selected fruit bowls. Shaw often begins discounting them around 7 p.m., though the specific cut-fruit combinations are not always selectable.

  • Clubs: unless you are part of the management team, joining clubs often feels more like paying fees than gaining meaningful experience. In my opinion, clubs are not a high priority.

  • Dorm life: buy a trash bin, a hanging bedside lamp, a laundry basket, moisture-proof door-seal tape, and a lock.

  • Laundry: avoid using the dryer for sensitive clothing if possible. Even though underwear is nominally prohibited in laundry rooms, many people still dry socks, underwear, and even shoes there. So I strongly recommend hand-washing underwear. Mixing close-contact garments with socks in shared machines may create hygiene risks.

  • Medical care: the Lower Campus community clinic does not operate 24 hours a day. In emergencies, students should call 120 directly rather than relying on the clinic phone. The clinic also seems reluctant to issue prescription medication, so it cannot substitute for proper hospital care.

  • Weather: always carry an umbrella. In southern China, especially from April to September, weather forecasts are often unreliable.

  • Student ID cards: final exams strictly require a student ID card. If yours is lost, you must fill out forms on site and then, within 48 hours, go to the CITIC Bank branch in the sunken plaza to replace it.sunken plaza to replace it (for 30 RMB). Surprisingly, other IDs such as national ID cards, passports, driver’s licenses, or electronic student IDs do not substitute. The replacement card may not become active immediately. Missing the 48-hour window can create serious administrative trouble.改正。

  • Course registration and Add/Drop week: the SIS registration system has an obvious design flaw. Although students who skip preselection are penalized by a delayed start time, there is still no requirement that preselection reflect real demand.e is no requirement that preselection reflect real demand. As a result, many people preselect only a few placeholder courses and then add many more in the formal round, badly distorting the school’s demand estimates.开通选修课（CEC、ENG、CHI 等）的抢课。

    Add/Drop week usually has two stages. On the first day or within the first two days of the semester, the registry opens any remaining seats for normal add-course operations. Later in that week, online petition forms begin. It is wise to speak with the target professor in advance, because professors have major influence over whether those forms are approved.

  • Taking SDS as an example: courses such as BIO1008, ENG1001, and CEC1000 are automatically assigned by the registry after course selection, so they cannot be manually added on sis.cuhk.edu.cn. SDS students usually only need to select items such as PED, CSC, MAT, and CHI themselves. “Courses Assigned by Registry Office: Course BIO1008, ENG1001, CEC1000# will be assigned by Registry after course registration. Additionally, for Columbia Class students, MAT1011 will also be assigned by Registry after course registration.” Operational instructions for other disciplines are generally distributed through Outlook email. Starting from the second semester, students are often expected to manage registration details mostly on their own.

  • For the national-conditions education series, namely CEC (1000–2000), note that after grades are released, email may provide a limited-time option to convert the course into free-elective credit so that it does not count toward GPA.

  • Keep records of your discussions with professors. Every professor belongs to particular academic and national networks and may have resources for sending students to specific projects. Recommendation letters should be personalized. Resume = basic-info + research interest/technical interest + what can I contribute. In a resume, the key is not to express admiration for the school’s prestige or the professor’s fame, but to communicate genuine enthusiasm for the project itself.

  • anything with enough data will be automated What about medicine, gene, and videotape? How can we make use of these massive bodies of data?

  • Learning ultimately depends on self-study, and self-study depends on interest.

  • Rough path: ~/School/Course/Stream.

  • CSE leans more toward algorithms and thought-style experimentation (for example optimization, methodology, programming, and machine learning), whereas ECE leans more toward machines and the physical world (for example drones, communication technology, and chips).

  • Research means reading references, talking to people, exchanging information, and then getting your hands dirty.

  • How to get into a research group:

    1. Start with the professors who teach your classes. Graduate students are expected to produce output; undergraduates must first show seriousness. What should a student do? The process is not only about academic ability. Apprenticeship takes time: show up, attend large-group meetings and smaller meetings with senior students, and keep doing so consistently.
    2. Department heads and class advisors.
    3. Dean reception days — but bring something sensible to discuss.

  • Suggested third- and fourth-year electives for CSE/DS students: CSC4140 and DDA4310 by Sun Qi Lin (difficult, but graded generously, with no exam and no attendance check). Courses I would personally avoid: DDA3003 and CSC3100 by Liu Mengmeng (overly granular grading, heavy workload buildup, and in 3100 attendance may even reduce rather than help your score).
